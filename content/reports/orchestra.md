# Positioning and Messaging Summary  

Orchestra’s market narrative around AI is woven into its broader story of being a **modern, integrated communications firm**. Some key elements of their positioning and messaging include:

- **Vision of Integration:** Orchestra consistently presents itself as a **unified platform of diverse expertise** built to handle contemporary challenges. The tagline of being *“built for today’s complex media landscape”* implies a readiness for whatever complexity entails – explicitly including AI. The complexity is defined by fragmentation of channels, speed of news cycles, and technological disruption (AI is a big part of that disruption). Thus, the core narrative is that **Orchestra can navigate and orchestrate across all these elements (story, data, digital) to capture attention** ([world.einnews.com](https://world.einnews.com/pr_news/705526314/berlinrosen-holdings-rebrands-as-orchestra#:~:text=Orchestra%20offers%20strategic%20communications%20services,reporting%20and%20digital%20marketing%20intelligence)). The term “Orchestra” itself is metaphorical – multiple instruments (agencies) playing in concert. In messaging, they hint that **AI and data analytics are new instruments** added to the mix that they can conduct adeptly. 

- **AI as Part of Innovation Theme:** Orchestra frames AI under the umbrella of innovation and evolution in communications. They don’t isolate AI as a separate pillar in branding; instead it appears alongside other innovative capabilities (like analytics, digital marketing). For example, the phrase from Rosen: *“industry quickly evolves and innovates with AI and data analytics tools”* ([www.prnewswire.com](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/berlinrosen-holdings-is-now-orchestra-302098315.html#:~:text=,AI%20and%20data%20analytics%20tools)) ties AI to innovation (not to fear or hype). Likewise, in its list of services, **AI is implicit** in terms like “digital marketing intelligence” ([world.einnews.com](https://world.einnews.com/pr_news/705526314/berlinrosen-holdings-rebrands-as-orchestra#:~:text=measurement%20and%20reporting%20and%20digital,marketing%20intelligence)). They rarely use “AI” in client-facing copy on their website except where relevant by context (Inkhouse’s page where AI is the industry focus). This suggests they position AI as **one important driver of change that Orchestra is adept in**, rather than the singular focus. 

- **Strategic Counsel Emphasis:** The messaging repeatedly emphasizes *strategy, counsel, expertise, leadership.* For instance, Orchestra’s LinkedIn description (from BerlinRosen’s page) says it *“combines executive advisory, strategy development, and tactical execution across channels”* with 700+ experts across sectors ([www.linkedin.com](https://www.linkedin.com/company/berlinrosen#:~:text=philanthropy%2C%20issue%20advocacy%2C%20labor%2C%20real,sports%2C%20travel%2C%20hospitality%20and%20more)). They are not framing themselves as just content producers or data crunchers – those are enablers for their counsel. The recurring use of **“top storytellers and strategists”** ([world.einnews.com](https://world.einnews.com/pr_news/705526314/berlinrosen-holdings-rebrands-as-orchestra#:~:text=Orchestra%20%28www,across%20North%20America%20and%20Europe)) is messaging to clients that with Orchestra, you get people who understand the big picture of AI’s impact on stakeholders and can craft the story accordingly.

They then posit themselves as having the solution via integrated teams. The *“storytelling engines”* phrasing by Derris ([world.einnews.com](https://world.einnews.com/pr_news/705526314/berlinrosen-holdings-rebrands-as-orchestra#:~:text=%E2%80%9CWe%20are%20creating%20storytelling%20engines,%E2%80%9D)) metaphorically links creative content with technology/AI fueling it. - **No Specific AI Tagline:** There is no single strapline like “Orchestra – orchestrating AI communications” (they didn’t go for a kitschy play on the name). The identity remains simply “Orchestra.” Any tagline like *“A new kind of communications company”* or similar (the LinkedIn snippet: *“guided by a philosophy about what it takes to succeed in an ever-changing media world”*) ([www.linkedin.com](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/orchestraco_a-new-kind-of-communications-company-guided-activity-7177993436766064640-tZBs#:~:text=A%20new%20kind%20of%20communications,Your)) is broad. **Position vs Competitors:** (While not explicitly asked to compare, a subtle position emerges) Many big comms firms (Edelman, etc.) have launched branded AI initiatives or partnerships – Orchestra has been quieter but integrated. They position themselves through the **outcomes (trust, attention, narrative)** rather than technology per se, which differentiates them from firms shouting about their AI tools. This likely appeals to board-level clients who want to solve strategic problems (not just buy a flashy tool). 

In conclusion, Orchestra’s market positioning around AI is as a **strategically savvy, data-empowered communications partner** that seamlessly blends traditional storytelling with modern analytical insight. They present AI as one of the critical factors shaping communications today – a factor they are comfortable and competent with – but always stress that success comes from pairing AI/data with human creativity, strategic thinking, and an integrated approach. The narrative is essentially: *“We get the art and science of communications in the AI era – we’ll handle both for you.”*

# Bottom-Line Assessment  

**Orchestra’s AI posture is one of active integration and strategic focus.** The firm has **clearly invested in AI-oriented capabilities** – evidenced by its acquisition of tech communications specialists (Inkhouse, Glen Echo) and the establishment of a data/insights team using AI tools for analytics. Orchestra is **already applying AI internally** (for research, content drafting, and intelligence) with documented success in efficiency, while enforcing human oversight to maintain quality ([messagelabmedia.com](https://messagelabmedia.com/ai-in-content-marketing/#:~:text=We%20have%20also%20tested%20ChatGPT,school%20Googling)) ([messagelabmedia.com](https://messagelabmedia.com/ai-in-content-marketing/#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20at%20least%20not%20to)). With clients, it is **not selling AI tech solutions**, but rather leveraging its understanding of AI to provide high-value counsel and execution: from elevating AI startups’ profiles to steering established companies’ AI narratives and handling AI-related crises. The pattern of thought leadership (blogs, events) and service evolution (new AI clients, analytic offerings) suggests **Orchestra treats AI as a core component of its consultancy**, not a sidebar experiment. 

The firm appears to be building toward an even more data-enhanced model of communications – possibly developing proprietary insight products or more formalized AI-powered services – but evidence of that is still thin. What’s clear is **institutional commitment**: top leadership voices prioritize AI and data innovation ([www.prnewswire.com](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/berlinrosen-holdings-is-now-orchestra-302098315.html#:~:text=,AI%20and%20data%20analytics%20tools)), and hiring patterns show embedding of AI skills across roles. There is no sign of AI being just hype for Orchestra; instead, it’s becoming **standard operating procedure and a selling point**. 

Where the evidence is thinner is on formal partnerships or unique AI IP – Orchestra hasn’t rolled out a branded AI platform like some competitors, and any vendor partnerships are behind the scenes. Also, aside from internal blogs, there’s limited public detail on exactly how its proprietary analytics work (we infer much from service descriptions). But this doesn’t imply inaction; rather, Orchestra may be deliberately *understated*, integrating off-the-shelf AI effectively instead of publicizing flashy proprietary tech. 

The overall pattern indicates a **serious, steady commitment** to AI: Orchestra quickly incorporated AI into its internal workflow (by 2024) and reshaped its service mix to address AI communications needs (by 2025). This is not a superficial marketing ploy – it’s woven into their growth strategy and daily operations. At the same time, Orchestra remains **grounded in human expertise** – consistently framing AI as an enhancer of (not replacement for) its strategic and creative counsel. For a board evaluating competitive standing, Orchestra comes across as a firm that **recognizes the transformative potential of AI and is proactively using it to augment its consulting services**, all while positioning itself as a guide for clients navigating the AI-driven environment. In short, Orchestra is neither lagging in the AI wave nor blindly chasing it – it’s **riding it thoughtfully**, strengthening its advisory toolkit and market relevance with evidence-backed adoption and a clear-eyed narrative.
