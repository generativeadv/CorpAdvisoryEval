# Section 9: Bottom-Line Assessment

**Excellera’s AI posture** since 2023 can be characterized as a decisive and strategic integration of AI into its core business of corporate advisory:

- **Clearly Doing:** Excellera has **concretely built and deployed AI capabilities** to enhance its services. This is evidenced by the acquisition of a data science firm (Intwig) in 2024 ([www.engage.it](https://www.engage.it/agenzie/excellera-acquisisce-la-societa-di-data-intelligence-intwig-.aspx#:~:text=Nata%20dieci%20anni%20fa%20da,moderne%20tecniche%20di%20intelligenza%20artificiale)), the launch of a dedicated AI-powered unit (Excellera Intelligence) in early 2025 with defined offerings ([www.mrweb.com](https://www.mrweb.com/drno/news37942.htm#:~:text=In%20Milan%2C%20strategic%20communications%20company,as%20CEO%20of%20Excellera%20Intelligence)), and the rollout of proprietary tools (data lake, AI algorithms for media and policy analysis) used in client engagements ([www.mrweb.com](https://www.mrweb.com/drno/news37942.htm#:~:text=The%20new%20firm%2C%20Excellera%20Intelligence%2C,made%20research%20to)). Internally, the company has restructured teams and workflows to embed these AI-driven insights into its consulting processes (consultants working alongside data analysts) ([excelleragroup.com](https://excelleragroup.com/nasce-excellera-intelligence/#:~:text=fondata%20da%20Aldo%20Cristadoro%2C%20entrata,comunicazione%20digitale%20alle%20relazioni%20istituzionali)). Externally, it is **selling AI-enhanced advisory services** that tackle reputation management, stakeholder intelligence, and regulatory monitoring, showing up in pitches and deliverables as a differentiator.

- **What They May Be Building Toward:** All signs point to Excellera **doubling down on data and AI as a long-term competitive advantage**. The firm likely aims to develop an even more robust intelligence platform that could incorporate next-generation AI (e.g., more advanced predictive analytics, perhaps early use of generative AI for scenario simulation). By investing in a standalone brand (Excellera Intelligence) and its own web presence, Excellera might be laying groundwork to productize some solutions in the future (for instance, offering subscription-based intelligence reports or platforms to clients). They may also be building toward a reputation as the go-to advisor for data-informed corporate strategy in Europe, potentially expanding the AI team or forming new partnerships to cover more data sources or geographies. Given Cristadoro’s hint that *“we will tell, step by step, the next changes”* ([it.linkedin.com](https://it.linkedin.com/posts/aldo-cristadoro-6b477028_excelleraintelligence-dataintelligence-activity-7289602940816822273-mxaU#:~:text=11%20mesi)), the company is likely planning iterative enhancements – possibly more automation in analysis, integration of AI insights with financial indicators, or even AI-driven scenario planning tools for clients (speculative, but logical next steps as they mature).

- **Where Evidence is Thin:** One area with sparse public evidence is **how extensively Excellera is using cutting-edge AI techniques like generative AI internally** beyond initial mentions. We infer some experimentation, but specifics (use of GPT for drafting, etc.) are not documented. Also, **client impact stories or metrics** are not yet publicly shared – we lack concrete case studies (e.g., “Using our AI, Client X averted a crisis” or “improved reputation score by Y%”). Additionally, there’s little detail on Excellera’s **AI governance and ethics policies**; while the firm likely has internal guidelines, there’s no explicit statement on how they manage AI bias, data privacy, or decision accountability. So, while we know the tools exist and are used, the exact *how* of their use (guardrails, training, performance statistics) is not fully transparent externally. Finally, hiring patterns beyond the acquisition are unclear – we don’t see evidence of a big talent ramp-up in AI roles post-launch, which could mean either the current team suffices or they are quietly hiring through networks.

- **Serious Commitment vs. Experimentation:** The pattern of actions strongly suggests a **serious institutional commitment** rather than a tentative experiment. Excellera invested capital in acquiring a company for AI skills ([www.engage.it](https://www.engage.it/agenzie/excellera-acquisisce-la-societa-di-data-intelligence-intwig-.aspx#:~:text=Intwig%2C%20societ%C3%A0%20di%20data%20intelligence,Advisors%20e%20Barabino%20%26%20Partners)), incorporated that company’s leadership into its own, and publicly rebranded part of its business around AI. These are heavy moves that go beyond superficial pilots. The timeline from late 2024 into 2025 shows sustained effort: integration, product development, launch, marketing, and thought leadership engagements – indicating that AI is now ingrained in Excellera’s strategy. The top executives’ consistent endorsement and the integration across all units imply that this is a company-wide transformation. It’s not confined to a small innovation lab on the side; it’s central to how Excellera delivers value. 

Indeed, by early 2026, Excellera appears to have **fully embraced AI as a core pillar** of its offerings, much like the incorporation of digital strategy in consultancies a decade prior. There is no sign of backtracking; if anything, Excellera might need to accelerate to stay ahead as others catch on, but it has the first-mover advantage in its niche. The evidence of client uptake or revenue attributable to AI isn’t public, but the board-level narrative (for which this research is prepared) would likely acknowledge Excellera as **one of the more advanced AI adopters in the communications advisory arena**.

In summary, Excellera Advisory Group has **moved assertively** to deploy AI both internally and in client services. They have a dedicated team and product (Excellera Intelligence) offering tangible data-driven solutions, and their leadership structure and messaging underscore a long-term, strategic commitment to AI. While certain details (e.g., internal policies, specific tech stack, client results) remain either private or in early stages, the overall pattern indicates that Excellera is not just experimenting at the edges – it has fundamentally integrated AI into its business model to enhance and differentiate its corporate affairs advisory services. This positions Excellera as a forward-leaning competitor in a field that is only beginning to adopt AI at scale, potentially giving it a significant edge in delivering informed, timely, and measurable advice to clients.
