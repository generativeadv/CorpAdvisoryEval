- They also maintain a **forward-looking optimism**: Richard Edelman’s commentary and others talk about AI’s leap forward akin to past tech leaps, implying optimism about innovation, but immediately couched in terms of proving value and responsibility ([www.edelman.com](https://www.edelman.com/trust/2025/trust-barometer/report-tech-sector#:~:text=The%202025%20Edelman%20Trust%20Barometer,society%20along%20for%20the%20journey)). This measured optimism is likely designed to appeal to corporate clients who have to be optimistic for shareholders but responsible in execution.
- There’s a strain of **empowerment narrative for communicators**: They often say tools are democratizing insights (from their report quote: “Gen AI has democratized access to insights... empowering a broader range of stakeholders” ([www.swordandthescript.com](https://www.swordandthescript.com/2024/10/edelman-generative-ai/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CGenerative%20AI%20has%20democratized%20access,making%2C%20further%20enhancing%20enterprise%20agility))). This positions Edelman somewhat as a champion for their core client base (communicators) to harness AI and remain valuable. It suggests: “We will help you, the communicator, not be left behind. With AI, you can do even more.” 

**No Competitor Comparison in Public Messaging:** 
- True to instruction and their general style, Edelman’s external messaging does not mention competitors by name. At most, an Edelman exec might generalize that “our competitors in holding companies struggle to integrate AI at speed” (as Buchwald hinted) ([europeantech.news](https://europeantech.news/how-edelman-is-ramping-up-investment-in-generative-ai/#:~:text=Buchwald%20said%20Edelman%20benefits%20from,within%20holding%20companies%2C%20he%20said)). But outwardly to clients, Edelman sells positive attributes of itself rather than negative of others. Their unique framing around trust likely serves as a thinly veiled differentiator (implying others might focus on tech without considering trust, but Edelman does).
- The one internal mention we saw (Europeantech article) of competitors was in context of holding company constraints ([europeantech.news](https://europeantech.news/how-edelman-is-ramping-up-investment-in-generative-ai/#:~:text=Buchwald%20said%20Edelman%20benefits%20from,within%20holding%20companies%2C%20he%20said)), but that’s more of an industry commentary than marketing copy.

**Geopolitical/Policy Tone:** 
- Because Edelman deals globally, their messaging also touches on geopolitical differences in AI perception. For instance, citing China’s higher trust vs US skepticism ([www.edelman.com](https://www.edelman.com/trust/2025/trust-barometer/report-tech-sector#:~:text=The%20Trust%20Landscape%3A%20A%20Global,Perspective)) positions Edelman as globally aware. This appeals to multinationals who need a nuanced approach in different markets – implicitly saying Edelman can counsel you differently in different regions given our insights.
- On policy, Edelman likely advocates for **responsible innovation** – not slowing AI to a halt, but implementing guardrails to maintain public trust (this is gleaned from their trust imperative messaging). They do not take extreme stances in messaging (like calling for a pause on AI development or, conversely, unregulated development). The positioning is moderate, aligning with the idea that *with the right principles (transparency, inclusion), AI can be a net positive* – and Edelman will ensure those principles are applied.

**Consistent Messaging Internally and Externally:** 
- Internally, Edelman’s messaging to employees would mirror what they say publicly: that using AI responsibly will make their work more powerful and predictive ([www.prweek.com](https://www.prweek.com/article/1863427/progress-report-agencies-using-ai-right#:~:text=On%20behalf%20of%20clients%2C%20the,of%20our%20comms%2C%E2%80%9D%20says%20Buchwald)), and that Edelman is leading by example in the industry. Likely Richard Edelman and other leaders frequently discuss AI in internal town halls, reinforcing that it’s core to strategy, which then translates to the authentic enthusiasm they show externally.

Overall, Edelman’s market positioning on AI is one of a **trusted navigator** at the intersection of technology, communications, and society. They strive to own concepts like “trustworthy AI adoption” and “AI for communications transformation,” thus carving a space where they can lead and not compete head-to-head on pure tech prowess. The messaging consistently ties back to how Edelman creates value: by building trust, shaping narratives, and leveraging intelligence – now turbocharged by AI, yet guided by human insight.
