# Bottom-Line Assessment

- **Clearly Doing:** FleishmanHillard is **deeply integrating AI into both its client services and internal operations**. The firm has launched concrete AI-powered offerings (e.g. Sage for audience insights, Risk Radar for issues monitoring) and built an internal platform (FH Fusion on Omni) to empower all employees with AI tools ([fleishmanhillard.com](https://fleishmanhillard.com/ai-powered-communications-solutions/#:~:text=Designed%20by%20our%20counselors%20for,methodologies%20and%20subject%20matter%20expertise)) ([fleishmanhillard.com](https://fleishmanhillard.com/ai-powered-communications-solutions/#:~:text=SAGE%20,%E2%80%8B)). It has trained its workforce to use generative AI in everyday tasks and created governance to do so responsibly ([fleishmanhillard.com](https://fleishmanhillard.com/tag/ai/#:~:text=%2A%20,but%20never%20replaces%20human%20judgment)). All evidence indicates AI is now a core component of how FH delivers insights, crafts strategies, and differentiates itself in the market.

- **Building Toward:** FH appears to be *building toward an agency model where data and AI-driven intelligence underpin every offering*. The trajectory – from initial data fluency programs to enterprise AI adoption – suggests FH is aiming to make **“intelligent consulting” its hallmark**. We can infer FH will continue developing bespoke AI agents for more areas (e.g. further personalization of content, predictive analytics for reputation). The investment in leadership (like a Head of AI Strategy, and tech architects) implies FH is setting foundations to scale these innovations globally and perhaps create new revenue streams from AI-enhanced advisory services. Essentially, FH is progressing toward a future where its communications counsel is continuously augmented by proprietary AI insights – offering clients faster, empirically-grounded, and even predictive counsel as a standard.

- **Evidence Gaps:** FH has been transparent about many initiatives, but some areas have limited visibility. There’s **little public data on the quantitative impact** of its AI usage (ROI, efficiency gains), making it hard to assess results versus rhetoric. No specific client case studies have been published attributing outcomes to FH’s AI tools – clients may be using them, but confidentiality keeps those wins private. Also, FH has not detailed any *external partnerships* in AI beyond Omnicom’s platform; if any exclusive alliances or technology investments exist, they haven’t been disclosed. Another subtle gap: while FH touts AI for strategy and analysis, it says less about using AI in creative content production (copywriting, design) – it likely does internally, but that narrative is downplayed, perhaps to avoid suggesting automation of the creative process. These areas of thin evidence mean we largely trust FH’s self-reported success without independent verification of how AI has improved outcomes.

- **Commitment vs. Experimentation:** The pattern of FH’s activities since 2023 signals **serious institutional commitment** rather than casual experimentation. The firm has dedicated budget and personnel (global MD-level roles, new SVPs) to AI, rolled out formal programs, and consistently messaged AI as central to its strategy ([fleishmanhillard.com](https://fleishmanhillard.com/tag/ai/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CEJ%E2%80%99s%20appointment%20reinforces%20our%20commitment,%E2%80%9D)) ([fleishmanhillard.com](https://fleishmanhillard.com/tag/ai-readiness/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CFH%20Fusion%20closes%20the%20innovation,%E2%80%9D)). This is not a one-off pilot or marketing gimmick – it’s multi-year and enterprise-wide. The fact FH’s work earned industry awards and external media spotlight suggests its peers and clients also see it as genuinely leading, not just talking. In contrast to agencies that might mention AI in passing, FH has operationalized it. All signs – breadth of usage, top-down and bottom-up involvement, embedded tools – point to FH treating AI as a long-term transformation of the firm’s capabilities and value proposition. In summary, FH’s posture is one of **proactive innovation**, positioning itself ahead of the curve in adopting AI, and doing so in a methodical, responsible manner aligned with its communications expertise.
